What Is a Workflow Audit?

Every engagement at Sidecar Advisory starts the same way: a free 45-minute working session that ends with a written 6-page audit of where your team is losing time — and what it's worth to fix it.

June 2026 · 6 min read · By Neil Barris, Founder

A Workflow Audit is a free 45-minute working session where Neil Barris of Sidecar Advisory maps the recurring admin work inside a small professional service firm — insurance, legal, accounting, or real estate — and identifies at least $5,000 in annual savings opportunities. You leave with a written 6-page audit either way, whether or not we ever work together again.

The hardest part of adopting AI as a small business isn't the technology — it's the paralysis. There are hundreds of tools, everyone has an opinion, and the stakes feel high enough that most owners end up doing nothing while waiting for the "right" moment to start. That moment rarely comes on its own.

The Workflow Audit is designed to break that paralysis. In one short, focused session, you go from "I know I should be using AI but I don't know where to start" to a specific, prioritized, written plan for your exact business. Here's what it involves and what you can expect.

Why start with an audit instead of a tool?

Because the tool is never the problem — implementation is. Most small businesses that get burned on AI didn't pick a bad tool; they automated the wrong workflow, or automated a messy process instead of cleaning it up first.

The audit flips the order. Before recommending anything, we figure out where your team actually loses time every week: the quote follow-ups that sit for hours, the renewal reminders someone does by hand, the intake forms retyped into three systems, the month-end reports assembled from scratch. Only then does it make sense to talk about which tools fit.

What happens during the 45 minutes?

The session is a structured working conversation, not a demo and not a pitch. We walk through your operations area by area — client communication, scheduling, intake, billing, reporting — and probe for the workflows that take the most time or generate the most friction.

We ask questions most business owners haven't been asked before. Not "what tools do you use?" but "what do you do the same way every single week?" Not "are you interested in AI?" but "if you could eliminate one task from your week, what would it be?" Those questions surface the opportunities that aren't obvious from the outside.

If you want a head start, the savings calculator gives you a rough estimate in 90 seconds — live audits typically find 30–60% more, because they capture context a calculator can't.

What's in the written 6-page audit?

Within 48 hours of the session, you receive the written audit. It isn't a generic AI overview — it's specific to your business. It includes:

  • Your highest-impact workflows to automate, ranked by estimated annual savings and ease of implementation
  • The hours per week each one currently costs you, based on what we walked through together
  • Specific tool recommendations for each workflow, with notes on why they fit your situation and what they cost
  • A suggested sequence — what to fix first, second, and later
  • Implementation notes for the first workflow, so you have a concrete starting point

This document is yours. You can take it to another provider, implement it yourself, or use it as the foundation for working with us further. There's no lock-in, and the plan is designed to be actionable regardless of what you decide next.

How does the $5,000 guarantee work?

Simple: if the audit doesn't identify at least $5,000 in annual savings opportunities for your business, you still get the full written workflow review — free, no strings. Since the session itself costs nothing, the worst case is that you spend 45 minutes and walk away with a professional, written review of your operations.

That guarantee reflects how we think about the engagement. The audit only works if we actually understand your business and give you findings you can use. If we can't find meaningful savings, we haven't earned the right to propose anything further.

What does a Workflow Audit cost?

Nothing. The Workflow Audit is free — it's the front door to everything else Sidecar Advisory does. Some audits turn into implementation projects, some turn into ongoing advisory relationships, and some end with the written audit and a handshake. All three outcomes are fine. You can see exactly what the paid engagements cost on our pricing page — no "contact us for pricing" anywhere.

What happens after the audit?

That's up to you. Many owners use the written audit to implement their first workflow on their own and see results within 30 days. Others move into the AI Starter Pack — a focused 30-day implementation where we build the first workflows together. And for firms that want ongoing support as the AI landscape changes, the AI Co-Pilot retainer keeps an advisor in your corner month to month.

But none of that is required. The audit stands on its own.

The most expensive AI decision a small business can make is spending months on the wrong tool. A 45-minute audit costs you nothing and gets you pointed in the right direction from the start.

How do I book a Workflow Audit?

Pick a time that works for you — the whole process is one 45-minute video call, and the written audit arrives within 48 hours. If you'd rather start smaller, run the 90-second savings calculator first, or browse the FAQ for answers about cost, timelines, and what to expect.

45 minutes. We find $5,000+ of admin worth automating — or you get the written audit anyway. No pressure either way.
